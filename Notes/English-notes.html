Reading I

Answer the following questions.

  • a. What do you know about artificial intelligence (AI)? Artificial intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think like humans and mimic their actions. It can involve capabilities like learning, problem-solving, and decision-making. As an example from the lesson, driverless cars use AI to navigate and operate.

  • b. How do you think AI has changed the world? AI has significantly changed the world by automating tasks, transforming industries, and altering how we interact with technology. For instance, the development of driverless technology, as discussed in the reading, has the potential to revolutionize commuting, improve safety, and make cities more sustainable.

Writing I

The use of AI in technology is not only making people's lives easier but bringing new challenges. Write a newspaper article about the challenges brought by the advancement of AI in technology.

To write this newspaper article, you should:

  1. Headline: Create a catchy headline related to AI challenges.
  2. Introduction: Briefly introduce AI and mention its dual nature (benefits and challenges), focusing on the challenges.
  3. Body Paragraphs: Dedicate paragraphs to specific challenges. Based on the lesson's theme of driverless cars and the broader context of AI, you could discuss:
    • Job displacement: As AI automates tasks (like driving), what happens to human jobs?
    • Safety and Security: Referencing the driverless car article, discuss the complexities of ensuring AI is safe in unpredictable human environments. Mention issues like hacking or AI making errors.
    • Ethical Dilemmas: Who is responsible when an AI system makes a mistake (e.g., a driverless car accident)? The article mentions the insurance industry grappling with this.
    • Over-reliance and loss of human skills.
    • Bias in AI: AI systems learn from data, and if the data is biased, the AI can perpetuate those biases.
    • Infrastructure and Regulation: The driverless car text mentions challenges in regulation and improving infrastructure.
  4. Conclusion: Summarize the main challenges and perhaps offer a forward-looking statement about the need for careful development and management of AI technology.

Example:

The Double-Edged Sword: Navigating the Challenges of AI Advancement

By [Your Name/A Staff Reporter]

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is rapidly transforming our world, promising unprecedented efficiency and innovation, from the way we commute to how industries operate. The allure of technologies like driverless cars, which can navigate our streets with sophisticated sensors and algorithms, is undeniable. However, as AI systems become increasingly integrated into the fabric of our society, they bring a host of complex challenges that demand our urgent attention. While AI offers solutions to many existing problems, its advancement is a double-edged sword, presenting new hurdles we must overcome to ensure a beneficial and equitable future.

One of the most immediate concerns is the potential for widespread job displacement. As AI-powered machines and software become capable of performing tasks previously done by humans – from driving vehicles to complex data analysis – many fear for their livelihoods. The dream of a "calm and serene way of getting from A to B" in a driverless vehicle might also mean a future with fewer employment opportunities for human drivers. This technological shift necessitates proactive strategies for reskilling and upskilling the workforce, alongside a broader societal conversation about the future of work itself.

Safety and security in an AI-driven world also present significant challenges. For AI systems like driverless cars to become mainstream, they must "operate safely and effectively in complex and unpredictable human environments". This is a monumental task, as these systems need to cope with variables like "human drivers speeding" or breaking road rules. Beyond physical safety, the risk of malicious attacks or inherent biases in AI algorithms leading to unfair or harmful outcomes is a growing concern. Ensuring that AI is not only intelligent but also robust, secure, and ethically sound is paramount.

Furthermore, the question of liability and responsibility in the age of AI is a complex legal and ethical minefield. As Richard Jinks from Oxbotica highlighted, the insurance industry is already grappling with the shift "from a person being responsible and in charge to the vehicle doing that". When an autonomous system makes an error, who is to blame? The programmer, the manufacturer, the owner, or the AI itself? Establishing clear regulatory frameworks and ethical guidelines is crucial to address these issues of accountability and ensure that the deployment of AI does not erode human responsibility.

The challenge also extends to public perception and trust. For technologies like driverless vehicles to be widely accepted, extensive testing and transparent communication about their capabilities and limitations are essential. Building this trust requires addressing not only the technological hurdles but also the societal anxieties surrounding AI. Moreover, the significant infrastructure changes and regulatory updates needed to support widespread AI adoption, as alluded to in the context of driverless technology, represent substantial logistical and financial challenges for governments and industries alike.

In conclusion, while the advancement of AI in technology opens doors to a more efficient and potentially safer future, it is imperative that we approach these innovations with a clear understanding of the accompanying challenges. Addressing concerns related to job displacement, ensuring robust safety and security measures, establishing ethical and legal frameworks for responsibility, and fostering public trust are critical steps. Only by proactively and thoughtfully navigating these complexities can we hope to harness the full potential of AI for the betterment of society, ensuring that this powerful technology serves humanity as a whole.
